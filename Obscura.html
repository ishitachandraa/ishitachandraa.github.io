Obscura: A hat full of surprises

A wearable magic hat that combines the joy of bubbles with a speech-activated card reveal, creating an enchanting experience for kids' magic shows.

Concept Development


Tangible Construction


The development process for Obscura began with building and testing the circuit. After ensuring the components worked as intended, I soldered specific wires to extend their length for easier integration into the design.

To make the 3D-printed box for the bubble mechanism waterproof, I painted it with nail polish. The bubble disk was securely glued to the DC motor, which was then mounted onto the lid of the box. Another DC motor with a fan attachment was hot-glued in place to blow the bubbles effectively.

This entire system was carefully positioned on top of the hat, with the breadboard glued to the side for accessibility and the touch sensor mounted on the brim for easy activation. To achieve a clean and cohesive look, I covered the setup with a cut-up black balaclava from Dollarama, which concealed the components while maintaining the hat’s traditional aesthetic.

For the finishing touches, I added a ribbon around the hat and incorporated fairy lights to enhance the magical appearance, ensuring that Obscura not only performed its tricks but also looked like an innocent traditional magic hat the audience.

3D Print Files

The 3D-printed box and lid used in the bubble mechanism were custom-designed by me to fit my specific needs. The bubble disk design, however, was adapted from this Instructables project, which provided a simple and effective solution for creating continuous bubbles.

Final Artifact



When I think of joy, I’m reminded of the magic shows I loved as a child—it wasn’t just the tricks themselves that were exciting, but the entire showmanship that made them unforgettable. With Obscura, I set out to combine that sense of wonder and theatricality with modern technology, crafting a wearable magic hat that captivates young audiences with interactive, imaginative performances.

Obscura is powered by an Arduino and combines two intuitive inputs—a speech command and a touch button—each unlocking a magical output. A secret phrase activates a servo motor to disclose a hidden card, creating a moment of surprise and delivering a dramatic "big reveal.". A touch of a button triggers a motorized bubble system, filling the air with playful, floating bubbles that bring a sense of enchantment and delight.

Obscura fits naturally into the magician’s outfit, building on the iconic image of the magic hat to create an engaging and interactive experience. While staying true to its traditional design, it integrates modern technology to surprise and delight. Obscura transforms a classic element of magic into a dynamic tool for evoking wonder, making it a perfect addition to any performer’s act.

Project Code

The project consists of two main code files: a Python script and an Arduino INO file. The Python script uses a speech recognition library to detect specific phrases such as a magic command ("read your mind"). When the command is detected, it sends signals to the Arduino through a serial connection. The Arduino INO file reads these signals to control a micro servo to reveal a card. The Arduino code also integrates a touch sensor and controls two DC motors to activate the bubble system.
